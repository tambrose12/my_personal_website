Hello, world!

My name is Taylor Ambrose

I am an aspiring Software Engineer looking to make a big life change. After working in the mortgage industry for over 6 years, I decided it is time to take a leap and persue an industry that has piqued my interest since my college years back in 2016. Making a big change like this is a bit outside my confort zone, but I am eager to learn all that I can and have a great support system in place. A loving husband that always has my back and wants the best for me, friends in the industry that are willing to help when I feel clueless, and a supportive family cheering me on for this next chapter in my life.


A Little About Me...

I was born in a tiny town in western New York state, about an hour or two south of Buffalo. I attended Jamestown Community College where I received my Associates Degree in Individual Studies. The day I graduated from college, May 21st, 2016, was also the day I married my best friend from high school. A week after my graduation and marriage, we had packed up everything we owned and our dog, Thor, and moved to South Carolina where some of my family was already living. I had considered trying to go to college for Computer Science, but we were in the process of buying our first home, money was in low supply, and I needed a job at the time.

While in the process of buying our first home, the Mortgage Loan Officer we were working with seemed to think I was picking up on the mortgage process fairly quickly and easily. He knew I needed a job, and they needed employees, so he offered me a position that I happily accepted. There was a lot to learn and a lot of engaging aspects of the industry, and I was able to work my way up in terms of pay and position fairly quickly. Anyone who has ever worked in the mortgage industry can tell you it has a way of drawing people in and they rarely ever escape. After three years in the industry, I was promoted to a Mortgage Underwriter position which I held for the next three years. It felt like a blink of an eye and six years had passed in an industry I had never meant to fall into (though I do not necessarily regret falling into it - I met a lot of great people and was able to build a stable life with that career).

All of that said, the I felt for years like I was missing some level of fulfillment in my career. I would love to be able to put my skills to use in a field that can involve a little more creativity... a satisfaction of building things... watching something come together.

I took a wild leap...

After working hard for years to save up money and reach a place where I could circle back and persue a career in computer science, or software engineering, I quit my comfortable job as an Underwriter in January 2023 to focus on this new path. I had already been working on some self-paced learning, but it was time to take the full leap and dive into this new field. Change can be scary, but so can a life of regret of having never tried something.
